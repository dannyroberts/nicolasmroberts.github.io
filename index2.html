I've spent most of that time working on a large granitic dome in the Pilbara Craton, Western Australia, the Mt Edgar granitic complex. The geodynamics that drove the construction of earth's Earliest continents (that's right, the Pilbara craton has rocks as old as 3.6 billion years old) is a matter of ongoing debate--was plate tectonics already operating at a global scale, or did these early continents form in some pre-plate tectonics context?

But I have also spent a lot of time working on the statistical treatment of high-dimensional structural geology data (like foliation-lineation pairs, strain ellipsoids). Structural geology is on the edge of a very exciting time--we are transitioning from using almost exclusively small field datasets........etc.

Statistical analysis scripts

From The utility of statistical analysis in structural geology (Roberts et al., in review)

Download the manuscript and the figures.

This paper analyzes three datasets: two from the West Mountain area of the western Idaho shear zone, and one from the Orofino area of the western Idaho shear zone, locally the Ahsahka shear zone. The two datasets from West Mountain are a foliation dataset and a dataset of foliation-lineation pairs. The data from Orofino are foliation-lineation pairs.

Links: The .zip file for the R scripts and .csv data files used for analysis can be downloaded here. Note that the these scripts require the programing language R along with the geologyGeometry package of statistical analysis functions written by Joshua R. Davis.
